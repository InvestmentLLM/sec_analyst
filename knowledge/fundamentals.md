# Fundamental Analysis Framework

## Valuation Benchmarks
- P/E below 15 = potentially undervalued; 15-25 = fair; above 25 = growth premium priced in
- Price/Book below 1 = potential value; 1-3 = normal; above 3 = intangible-heavy or premium brand
- EV/EBITDA below 8x = cheap; 8-15x = fair; above 20x = expensive unless high growth
- PEG ratio below 1.0 = potentially undervalued relative to growth

## Profitability Benchmarks
- Gross Margin: <20% = commodity; 20-40% = average; 40-60% = strong; >60% = exceptional (software/pharma)
- Operating Margin: <5% = thin; 5-15% = average; 15-25% = strong; >25% = excellent
- Net Margin: <5% = thin; 5-10% = average; 10-20% = good; >20% = excellent
- ROE: <10% = poor; 10-15% = average; 15-20% = good; >20% = excellent (check if inflated by debt)
- ROIC above cost of capital = value creation; below = value destruction

## Revenue Growth Benchmarks
- Declining: negative growth = serious concern
- Stagnant: 0-3% = mature business, limited upside
- Moderate: 3-8% = steady, value-oriented
- Good: 8-15% = healthy growth
- Strong: 15-25% = high growth, watch profitability
- Hypergrowth: >25% = exciting but often priced expensively

## Balance Sheet Health
- Current Ratio below 1.0 = potential liquidity risk; 1.0-2.0 = healthy; above 2.0 = conservative
- Debt/Equity below 0.3 = low leverage; 0.3-1.0 = moderate; 1.0-2.0 = leveraged; above 2.0 = high risk
- Net Cash (cash minus total debt) positive = very healthy balance sheet
- Interest Coverage above 5x = comfortable; 2-5x = manageable; below 2x = danger zone

## Cash Flow Quality (critical — harder to fake than earnings)
- Free Cash Flow = Operating Cash Flow minus CapEx
- FCF consistently exceeding Net Income = high earnings quality
- FCF Margin above 10% = strong cash generation
- OCF to Net Income ratio above 1.0 = clean earnings; below 0.7 = potential earnings manipulation
- CapEx as % of Revenue: below 5% = asset-light model; 5-15% = moderate; above 15% = capital-intensive

## Red Flags to Always Check
- Revenue declining two or more consecutive years
- Net income growing while operating cash flow is flat or declining (earnings quality issue)
- Goodwill exceeding 50% of total assets (acquisition-heavy, impairment risk)
- Accounts receivable growing significantly faster than revenue
- Gross margin declining consecutively for 2+ years
- Long-term debt growing while revenue stagnates
- Large gap between GAAP earnings and "adjusted" earnings cited by management
- Going concern language in auditor notes

## Earnings Quality Indicators
- Cash conversion ratio (OCF/Net Income) above 1 = high quality
- Low accruals as % of revenue = better earnings quality
- Consistent earnings with low revision history = trustworthy management
- Revenue recognition changes in recent filings = potential manipulation signal

## Scoring Sub-Category Guidance
- Revenue Growth (0-20): Declining=0-4; Stagnant=5-9; Moderate=10-13; Good=14-17; Strong=18-20
- Profitability (0-20): Loss-making=0-3; Thin margins=4-8; Average=9-13; Strong=14-17; Excellent=18-20
- Balance Sheet (0-20): Distressed=0-3; Weak=4-8; Average=9-13; Healthy=14-17; Fortress=18-20
- Earnings Quality (0-15): Poor=0-4; Below average=5-8; Average=9-11; Good=12-13; Excellent=14-15
- Outlook (0-15): Deteriorating=0-4; Uncertain=5-8; Stable=9-11; Positive=12-13; Excellent=14-15
- Risk Profile (0-10): High risk=0-2; Elevated=3-4; Moderate=5-6; Low=7-8; Very low=9-10
